**By Executive Chef Cheng Koung Meng**

Orchestrating a large-scale corporate event or a major wedding for 500 to 2,000 guests in Phnom Penh is not a test of culinary artistry; it is a hard logistical battle against time, space, and physical limits. Serving high-quality, hot meals to a thousand people simultaneously requires precise calculations and strict operational discipline. At CKM, we approach large-scale catering as an engineering problem, stripping away the guesswork to guarantee consistent execution from the first table to the last.

## 1. Venue Hard Limits: Electrical Load and Infrastructure

The foundation of a successful banquet is knowing the exact physical limits of the venue.
Long before a menu is finalized, CKM’s operations team conducts a comprehensive site audit. We do not look at the decor; we measure the facility's electrical load capacity, water access points, and drainage routes. If a venue’s temporary grid cannot support the massive power draw of our high-voltage ovens and thermal holding cabinets, the entire service is at risk. By identifying these bottlenecks early, we can deploy independent industrial generators and stabilize the infrastructure before the first piece of equipment is unloaded.

## 2. Menu Engineering Against Heat Loss

In high-volume catering, a menu must be designed to survive the journey from the staging kitchen to the furthest table in the room.
We engineer our menus based on thermal retention and environmental variables. Items requiring a crispy texture are assigned the absolute shortest service routes to prevent them from becoming soggy. Conversely, soups and braised dishes utilize their own viscosity as a natural thermal layer, protecting the core temperature against air-conditioning drafts or outdoor winds. Our menus are built to withstand the inevitable delays of event speeches, ensuring the food remains hot and structurally intact.

## 3. Manpower Routing and Bottleneck Prevention

Throwing a massive number of waitstaff at a large event only creates chaos and collisions.
Smooth service relies on strict spatial geometry. CKM divides the dining floor into specific service grids, calculating the exact walking distances for our staff. We establish dedicated, one-way service channels to prevent front-of-house staff from colliding with guests or each other. By mapping out these routes, we prevent food from sitting stagnant at the pass and maintain a continuous, rapid flow of service regardless of the room's size.

> A successful 100-table banquet is not the result of luck. It is built on precise calculations of electrical capacity, walking distances, and temperature control.

### Event Logistics: Frequently Asked Questions

**If we select an outdoor venue or an empty warehouse with no kitchen, will the food quality drop?**
No. For raw venues lacking basic infrastructure, CKM deploys fully self-sufficient mobile kitchens. We bring in independent, silent power generators, commercial-grade water filtration systems, and proper sanitation setups. By controlling the environment from the ground up, we ensure the cooking and holding capabilities match those of a permanent, standard commercial kitchen.

**How does the kitchen handle unexpected increases in the guest count on the day of the event?**
Guest counts at large events are rarely exact. CKM’s procurement protocol automatically includes a calculated percentage of buffer ingredients (extra tables). These reserves are kept in proper cold storage on-site. If the host confirms that the extra capacity is needed, our kitchen can immediately fire up these reserve ingredients without disrupting the timing or quality of the main service line.
